## 1. The original 1975 work — title, venue, and the actual wording

**Key finding: there was not one 1975 paper but TWO, and the famous crisp one-liner is a later crystallization, not a clean verbatim 1975 sentence.**

Goodhart first put the observation in print in **two** papers, both delivered at a **Reserve Bank of Australia conference in Sydney in July 1975**, and both published in *Papers in Monetary Economics*, Volume I, Reserve Bank of Australia, Sydney (1975):

- Goodhart, C.A.E. (1975), "Monetary Relationships: A View from Threadneedle Street"
- Goodhart, C.A.E. (1975), "Problems of Monetary Management: The U.K. Experience" (pp. 1–20)

Confirmed by Rodamar (2018, *Significance*): "Goodhart's first published formulation of his law appeared in 1975, in two papers on monetary relationships and management… They drew on his July 1975 papers at a Reserve Bank of Australia conference."

**On the exact wording:**

> "Any observed statistical regularity will tend to collapse once pressure is placed upon it for control purposes."

- The 1975 statement was a footnote/"jocular aside," not a stated "law." Chrystal & Mizen (2003) quote it in the self-referential form "Ignoring Goodhart's law, that any observed statistical regularity will tend to collapse once pressure is placed upon it for control purposes" — a **later** Goodhart restatement. They describe the original as "a (jocular) aside rather than the main point of the paper."
- The canonical crisp sentence is most reliably anchored to the **1984 reprint**: Goodhart, *Monetary Theory and Practice: The UK Experience* (Macmillan, 1984), **p. 96**.
- Secondary sources disagree on exact words ("collapse once pressure is placed upon" vs "break down when pressure is applied to") — the tell-tale sign of a reconstructed quotation.

**Safest citation:** "(Goodhart 1975; as restated in Goodhart 1984, p. 96)."

Authoritative scholarly treatment: **Chrystal, K.A. and Mizen, P.D. (2003), "Goodhart's Law: Its Origins, Meaning and Implications for Monetary Policy,"** in P. Mizen (ed.), *Central Banks, Monetary Theory and Policy: Essays in Honour of Charles Goodhart*, Vol. 1, Edward Elgar.

## 2. The monetary-policy context

- **Competition and Credit Control (Sept 1971):** lending controls liberalized → "a dramatic surge in bank intermediation, leading to broad money growth rates in excess of 25%" in 1972–73.
- **"The Corset" (Supplementary Special Deposits, from late 1973):** direct controls re-imposed; banks engineered around them (disintermediation) — Goodhart's point in microcosm.
- **MTFS (1980, Thatcher/Howe):** £M3 targeted at 7–11% for 1980/81, declining path.
- **The breakdown:** actual £M3 growth 1980/81 ≈ 18–19%, far above target, *even as* the economy went into deep recession and inflation fell. Broad-money targeting effectively abandoned by 1985–86 (Lawson). UK Treasury ministers themselves invoked the "law" to explain the overshoots.

## 3. The Strathern / Hoskin popularization chain

**Keith Hoskin (1996)** coined the naming and target-language paraphrase:
> "'Goodhart's Law' – That every measure which becomes a target becomes a bad measure – is inexorably, if ruefully, becoming recognized as one of the overriding laws of our times."
(Hoskin, in Munro & Mouritsen eds., *Accountability: Power, Ethos and the Technologies of Managing*, 1996, pp. 265–282.)

**Marilyn Strathern (1997, p. 308)**, verbatim:
> "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. The more a 2.1 examination performance becomes an expectation, the poorer it becomes as a discriminator of individual performances. Hoskin describes this as 'Goodhart's law', after the latter's observation on instruments for monetary control…"

(Strathern, "'Improving ratings': audit in the British University system," *European Review* 5(3): 305–321, 1997；改写自 1997-03-11 Girton College Founders' Memorial Lecture。)

**Goodhart's own reflection:** "it does feel slightly odd to have one's public reputation largely based on a minor footnote." — 出处指向 Goodhart (1997), "Whither Now?", *BNL Quarterly Review* 50: 385–430（经由维基传记的引注核验，未读原文，需图书馆二次核验）。

## Misattribution / myth flags

1. **MYTH:** "When a measure becomes a target…" 是 Goodhart 的话 → 假，是 Strathern (1997) 措辞、Hoskin (1996) 命名。
2. **MYTH:** 精炼句是 1975 年原文 → 不精确，锚点应为 1984 年书 p.96。
3. **MYTH:** 出自单篇 1975 论文 → 有两篇 RBA 会议论文。
4. **CAUTION:** RBA 文集出版年份 1975/1976 各目录说法不一（会议为 1975 年 7 月）。
5. **CAUTION:** 与 Campbell 定律是独立提出的近似原理；Rodamar (2018) 论证 Campbell 的核心要素发表更早（1969, 1975）。
6. **CAUTION:** 未找到"2004 年 Goodhart 自我反思"一说的出处；反思引语是 1997 年。
